### Jackie's Granola
*Page 4*

*(Recipe Usually Doubled)*

#### Ingredients
- 4 cups Old Fashioned Oats
- 1½ cups sliced Almonds
- ½ cup light Brown Sugar, packed
- ½ tsp Cinnamon
- ¼ tsp Kosher Salt
- ⅓ cup Coconut Oil*, Melted
- ⅓ cup Honey
- 2 tsp Vanilla Extract

#### Instructions
1. Heat oven to 300 degrees. Prepare a baking sheet by lining with parchment paper.
2. Mix the oats, almonds, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt together in a large mixing bowl until combined. In a small, separate bowl, stir the melted coconut oil, honey, and vanilla together, then drizzle over the oat mixture, stirring together until well-combined.
3. Transfer the granola mixture to the prepared baking sheet and spread it out in an even layer. Bake for 20-25 minutes, rotating the pan once halfway through for even toasting. Bake until golden and crispy and the almonds look lightly toasted (may take more than 25 minutes). Watch carefully as the granola can burn.
4. Remove from oven and let the granola cool completely before transferring to an airtight container. Keeps for 2 weeks at room temperature.

*Can substitute coconut oil with butter or vegetable oil, if preferred.

#### Ingredients
- 3 (13.5 oz) cans Coconut Milk (may substitute 4 cups of regular coconut milk or almond milk)
- 5 tablespoons Maple syrup
- ¼ teaspoon ground Cinnamon
- ⅛ teaspoon Sea Salt
- 12 tablespoons Chia seeds (about ¾ cup)
- 1 teaspoon Vanilla

#### Instructions

### Easiest Gingerbread French Toast
*Page 7*

**Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** 15 minutes | **Servings:** 12 slices

*Gingerbread French Toast is a festive holiday recipe that comes together in no time. Fry your brioche bread in a simple batter of eggs, milk, molasses, and cozy spices like ginger and cinnamon. The finished product is perfectly golden brown, never eggy or soggy, and makes the easiest, coziest brunch!*

#### Ingredients
- 1 loaf brioche bread (Use a 1 lb loaf - I recommend Trader Joe's Brioche)
- 4 large eggs
- 1 cup milk
- 2 tbsp real maple syrup
- 2 tbsp molasses
- 1 tsp ground ginger
- ½ tsp cinnamon
- ⅛ tsp nutmeg
- ⅛ tsp ground cloves
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- pinch of salt

#### Instructions
1. Whisk together eggs, milk, maple syrup, molasses, ginger, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, vanilla, and salt.
2. Slice brioche bread into thick slices.
3. Dip each slice in the batter, coating both sides.
4. Fry on a buttered griddle or pan until golden brown on each side.
5. Serve with fresh rosemary (optional), fresh cranberries (optional), butter, maple syrup, and powdered sugar (optional).

### Roasted Tomato Basil Soup
*Page 34*

**Source:** Food Network - The Barefoot Contessa Cookbook

#### Ingredients
- 3 pounds ripe plum tomatoes, cut in half
- ¼ cup plus 2 tablespoons good olive oil
- 1 tablespoon kosher salt
- 1½ teaspoons freshly ground black pepper
- 2 cups chopped yellow onions (2 onions)
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
- ¼ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
- 1 (28-ounce) canned plum tomatoes, with their juice
- 4 cups fresh basil leaves, packed
- 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
- 1 quart chicken stock, or water

#### Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F. Toss together the tomatoes, ¼ cup olive oil, salt, and pepper.
2. Spread the tomatoes in 1 layer on a baking sheet and roast for 45 minutes.
3. In an 8-quart stockpot over medium heat, saute the onions and garlic with 2 tablespoons of olive oil, the butter, and red pepper flakes for 10 minutes, until the onions start to brown.
4. Add the canned tomatoes, basil, thyme, and chicken stock.
5. Add the oven-roasted tomatoes, including the liquid on the baking sheet.
6. Bring to a boil and simmer uncovered for 40 minutes.
7. Pass through a food mill fitted with the coarsest blade.
8. Taste for seasonings. Serve hot.

### Easy Ham and Bean Soup
*Page 19*

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 30 minutes | **Servings:** 8

*This ham and white bean soup recipe is hearty, uses pantry staples, and is so simple and easy. It gets thick and creamy like the chowders! It's the best way to use up leftover ham.*

#### Ingredients
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- ½ cup medium onion (chopped finely)
- 1 large carrot peeled & chopped small
- 3 stalks celery (chopped)
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- ¼ teaspoon Italian seasoning
- ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
- 4 cups chicken broth
- 2 cups water
- 2 cups cooked ham (chopped for more to taste)
- 2 (14 ounce) cans white beans (drained)
- Salt & pepper to taste

#### Instructions
1. Heat the oil over medium-high heat in a large soup pot. Saute the onions, carrot, and celery 7-10 minutes, stirring occasionally (a little browning is good).
2. Stir in the garlic and cook for about 30 seconds.
3. Add the Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, chicken broth, water, ham, and white beans to the pot. Increase heat to high and bring to a gentle boil. Reduce heat to medium (or medium-low if it's bubbling too much) close the pot with the lid slightly ajar, and let the soup simmer for 20-25 minutes.
4. Season with salt & pepper to taste. Ham can be quite salty, so be sure to add salt after it's cooked.

*Optional: Take a potato masher and mash some of the beans right in the pot to give a slightly thicker texture or pulse an immersion blender a few times.*

### Velveeta Cheesy Broccoli Soup
*Page 20*

**Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 15 minutes | **Servings:** 6

*This Velveeta broccoli cheese soup recipe is the real deal. This thick, creamy broccoli Cheddar soup is ready in less than 30 minutes.*

#### Ingredients
- 2 tablespoons butter or margarine
- ¼ cup chopped onion
- 2 tablespoons flour
- 2 cups milk (add more if needed)
- ½ pound VELVEETA, cut up
- 1 (10 ounce) package frozen chopped broccoli, thawed, drained
- ⅛ teaspoon pepper

#### Instructions
1. Melt butter in a large saucepan on medium heat. Add onion; cook and stir until onion is tender, about 5 minutes. Add flour; cook 1 minute or until bubbly, stirring constantly.

*Page 21*

- 1 Tbsp chopped fresh parsley
- Salt and pepper to taste

#### Instructions

### Smoked Beef Barley Soup
*Page 32*

**Prep Time:** 15 minutes | **Cook Time:** 1 hour 15 minutes | **Servings:** 8

*A rich hearty soup using leftover smoked beef. Brisket, sirloin, or other smoked beef can work. Delicious comfort food for cold winter months.*

#### Ingredients
- 1 cup chopped onion
- 1 cup chopped celery
- 1 cup chopped carrots
- 2 tbsp butter
- 2 cups smoked beef, finely chopped
- 6 cups water
- 6 cups beef broth
- 1 (28 oz) can of diced tomatoes
- 1½ cup dry pearl barley
- 2 tsp salt
- 1 tsp ground black pepper

#### Instructions
1. In a large soup pot, cook onions, celery and carrots with the butter. Once the onions and celery are tender, about 6-8 minutes, add the beef.
2. Cook, stirring often, for another 5 minutes.
3. Add the water, beef broth and diced tomatoes. Bring to a boil and stir in the remaining ingredients.
4. Let the soup cook at a low rolling boil for 30-45 minutes. The barley should be fully cooked and the beef should be tender.

### Copycat Carrabba's Pollo Rosa Maria
*Page 15*

*This is a good crispy version of this delicious favorite. It is modified from a version found on Recipe Goldmine.*

#### Ingredients
- 4 boneless chicken breasts
- 8 slices prosciutto
- ¼ cup Fontina cheese
- ½ cup clarified butter
- 3 garlic cloves
- sweet onion, diced
- ½ cup dry white wine
- 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
- white pepper
- dash salt
- 8 ounces sliced cremini mushrooms
- ½ cup fresh basil, chopped
- lemon, juice of

#### Instructions
1. Open chicken breasts and lay them flat open side up. Grill 2-5 minutes on each side until the inside is done.
2. Remove one of the butterflied sides from each and stuff one of the sides with ½ oz cheese and top inside.
3. Fold to sandwich the filling inside.
4. Hold folded breasts with wooden picks.
5. Sear one side. Remove breast and cover.
6. In a large skillet saute butter and onions, add garlic in the clarified butter until tender.
7. Deglaze the pan with the white wine.
8. Add the butter, salt, and pepper, and mushrooms and cook until tender.
9. Add basil and lemon juice and swirl pan to combine.
10. Pour mushroom sauce over the Chicken breasts.

### Quiche
*Page 11*

*After 45 minutes, if quiche needs additional time, turn pan around in oven and continue baking until quiche is golden brown on top.*

#### Ingredients
- 16 Eggs
- 4 cups Half'n Half (or 1 whole quart)
- 2 cups Mayonnaise
- 8 Tablespoons Flour (½ cup)
- 1⅓ cup minced White Onion
- 1 tsp Garlic Powder (only 1 teaspoon!!)
- 1 tsp Salt (only 1 teaspoon!!)
- 4 cups shredded Swiss Cheese
- 2 cups Bacon bits
- 2 lb package Frozen Spinach (thawed and squeezed dry!)
- 4 deep dish pie shells thawed and unbaked
- Bacon Bits – for topping
- Swiss, Shredded – for topping

#### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
2. Whisk eggs in large bowl.
3. Add half & half, mayonnaise and flour to eggs. Mix well.
4. Add onion, garlic powder, salt, Swiss cheese, bacon and spinach.
5. Pour mixture into pie shells, dividing as evenly as possible.
6. Top with bacon bits and then more Swiss cheese.
7. Bake for 45 minutes or 1 hour until the top is golden brown.

### Gravy
*Page 10*

#### Ingredients
- 8 oz Bacon Fat
- 1 cup AP Flour (8 oz)
- 2 teaspoons Salt
- 2 teaspoons Pepper
- 6-8 cups milk

#### Instructions
1. Melt bacon fat on medium-low in large skillet or pot.
2. Let bacon fat begin to bubble/simmer before adding flour. Whisk in flour to prevent clumps.
3. Cook flour/fat mixture for 2 minutes, whisking occasionally.
4. Begin adding milk. Whisking as you add.
5. Continue whisking for 5 minutes to fully incorporate milk with flour.
6. Add salt and pepper and stir in thoroughly.
7. Bring mixture to a simmer, whisking occasionally.
8. If mixture becomes too thick, add ½ cup milk at a time whisking in thoroughly.
9. Continue with additional milk until gravy reaches desired consistency.

### French Dip Sauce (Au Jus)
*Page 23*

**DOUBLE THIS RECIPE**

#### Ingredients
- 8 Tablespoons Butter
- ⅓ Cup finely minced Red Onion
- 4 Teaspoons chopped Garlic
- 4 Tablespoons Flour
- 4 Cups Beef Broth
- 2 Tablespoons Red Wine Vinegar

#### Instructions
1. Melt butter into large sauce pot. Cook onion until translucent, then add garlic and saute 1 minute.
2. Stir flour into butter mixture and cook about 3-4 minutes or until light blond colored roux.
